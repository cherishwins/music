## PRODUCTION PATTERNS

### BPM/Tempo Ranges

| Style | BPM Range | Half-Time Feel | Notes |
|-------|-----------|----------------|-------|
| **Trap (Metro Boomin)** | 130-170 | 65-85 | Most common |
| **West Coast/Hyphy** | 95-105 | 47-52 | "Not Like Us" = 101 BPM |
| **Boom Bap** | 85-100 | - | Classic feel |
| **Drill** | 140-145 | 70-72 | Sliding 808s |

**Winner for Korea Crossover**: 90-105 BPM (emotional, allows complex flows)

### Key/Scale Patterns

| Key Type | Frequency | Mood |
|----------|-----------|------|
| **Minor Keys** | 85%+ | Dark, emotional, dramatic |
| **C# Minor** | Very common | Metro Boomin signature |
| **D♭/C# Major** | Hit example | "Not Like Us" |
| **A Minor, E Minor** | Classic choices | Universal feel |

**Winner for Korea**: Minor keys (matches K-hip-hop aesthetic)

### 808 & Bass Patterns

**Metro Boomin Style**:
- Deep, rumbling 808s
- Layered with complementary kick (not competing)
- Notes typically: C, D, G
- Moderate sustain with controlled distortion
- Sliding 808s for drill influence

**Spinz 808**: Industry standard sound

### Drum Patterns

**Kick**: On-beat with syncopated fills
**Snare/Clap**: 2 and 4, fingersnap layers common
**Hi-Hats**:
- 16th note base
- 32nd note rolls for tension
- Pitch variations (Metro signature)
- Triplet polyrhythms for complexity

### Melodic Elements

**2024 Trends** (from Hit Songs Deconstructed):
- **Synth (non-bass)**: 73% of top 10 hits
- **Acoustic drums/percussion**: 37% (5x increase since 2022!)
- **Electric bass**: 32% (up from 17% in 2020)
- **Piano**: Rising trend, emotional weight

**Atmospheric Elements**:
- Haunting pads
- Eerie textures
- Sample chops (soul/R&B sources)
- Orchestral stabs (strings, brass)

## "NOT LIKE US" CASE STUDY

**The #1 Song of 2024** - 21 weeks at top

### Technical Specs
- **BPM**: 101
- **Key**: C#/D♭ Major
- **Time Signature**: 4/4
- **Duration**: 4:34

### Production Breakdown
- **Producer**: Mustard (with Sounwave, Sean Momberger)
- **Creation Time**: ~30 minutes
- **Sample Source**: Monk Higgins cover of "I Believe to My Soul"

### What Made It Work
1. **Urgency**: Pitched-up sample created relentless energy
2. **Club-Friendly**: West Coast hyphy stylings
3. **Aggressive Tone**: Additional bassline, snare drums, finger snaps
4. **Cultural Moment**: Beef context drove conversation
5. **Danceability**: High energy, very danceable

### Crossover Elements for Korea
- Sample-based (K-hip-hop loves samples)
- Clear, aggressive delivery (translates well)
- Cultural reference density (controversy drives engagement)
- Memorable hook (singable even without understanding)

## LYRICAL PATTERNS

### Theme Distribution in Hits

| Theme | Percentage | Notes |
|-------|------------|-------|
| **Triumph/Flex** | 35% | "I made it" narrative |
| **Struggle/Real Talk** | 30% | Authenticity driver |
| **Beef/Competition** | 15% | 2024 spike (Kendrick/Drake) |
| **Relationships** | 10% | Less than pop |
| **Lifestyle** | 10% | Status, luxury |

**Korea Crossover**: Struggle → Triumph arc translates universally

### Hook Characteristics

**Successful Hooks**:
- Max 10 words typically
- One key memorable phrase
- Melodic element (not just rhythmic)
- Call-and-response potential
- Quotable/meme-able

**"Not Like Us" Hook**: "They not like us" - 4 words, infinitely repeatable

### Delivery Patterns

| Delivery Style | When Used |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Aggressive/Fast** | Verses, diss tracks |
| **Melodic/Sung** | Hooks, emotional moments |
| **Laid-back** | Flex verses, lifestyle content |
| **Strained/Emotional** | Struggle content |

## KOREAN CROSSOVER FACTORS

### Why K-Hip-Hop Works in US

1. **Production Quality**: Often higher than US indie
2. **Technical Skill**: Complex flows, fast delivery
3. **Emotional Authenticity**: Not just flex culture
4. **Visual Aesthetic**: Strong MV + fashion presence
5. **Fan Engagement**: Dedicated, organized fandoms

### US Elements That Work in Korea

1. **808-heavy production**: Universal appeal
2. **Emotional content**: Resonates strongly
3. **Melodic hooks**: K-pop influence
4. **High production value**: Expected baseline
5. **Storytelling**: Korean drama culture loves narrative

### Bridge Techniques

**Language Integration**:
- Korean verses + English hook
- Code-switching within bars
- Korean ad-libs in English songs
- Sound-alike phrases

**Cultural Markers**:
- Reference both cultures authentically
- Avoid stereotypes
- Collaborate with Korean artists
- Acknowledge hip-hop's Black American roots

## PRODUCTION TEMPLATES

### Template 1: Emotional Trap (Korea Bridge)
```yaml
bpm: 92-98
key: C# Minor or A Minor
808:
  style: sustained, slight distortion
  pattern: syncopated, melodic movement
drums:
  kick: on-beat with ghost notes
  snare: 2 and 4, layered clap
  hats: 16th with occasional 32nd rolls
melody:
  instruments: piano (primary), atmospheric pads
  style: melancholic, building
  samples: soul/R&B chops optional
structure:
  intro: 8-12 seconds, atmospheric
  verse: 30-45 seconds, restrained
  pre_hook: 15-20 seconds, building
  hook: 20-25 seconds, full production
dynamics:
  arc: quiet → building → explosive → resolution
```

### Template 2: Hard Trap (Metro Boomin Style)
```yaml
bpm: 140-145
key: C# Minor
808:
  style: deep rumble, Spinz 808
  pattern: sliding, aggressive
drums:
  kick: punchy, layered with 808
  snare: sharp crack, reverb tail
  hats: rapid rolls, pitch variations
melody:
  instruments: dark synths, orchestral stabs
  style: eerie, atmospheric
  samples: haunting vocals, bells
structure:
  intro: 15 seconds, building tension
  verse: 45 seconds, aggressive delivery
  hook: 30 seconds, full energy
dynamics:
  arc: tension → explosion → tension → explosion
```

### Template 3: West Coast/Hyphy (Not Like Us Style)
```yaml
bpm: 100-105
key: C# Major or D♭ Major
808:
  style: punchy, club-ready
  pattern: driving, consistent
drums:
  kick: four-on-floor influence
  snare: fingersnaps, claps
  hats: groovy, less trap rolls
melody:
  instruments: sampled strings, brass, piano
  style: urgent, sped-up samples
  samples: soul/R&B essential
structure:
  intro: short, immediate energy
  verse: 35-40 seconds, aggressive
  hook: 25-30 seconds, chant-able
dynamics:
  arc: high energy throughout, relentless
```
